I am building this archive around the way I actually study: careful debugging, repeatable notes, and a steady climb through **cybersecurity learning**, **system hacking**, and **developer tooling**.

This portfolio is designed to make three things easy to understand at a glance:

1. what I am working on,
2. how I reason about hard technical problems, and
3. what I learned after shipping or solving them.

### Focus Areas

- System hacking and binary exploitation fundamentals
- CTF-style challenge writeups with repeatable workflows
- Technical writing that turns trial-and-error into reusable notes
- Small developer-facing systems that stay maintainable under pressure

### Why a writeup-first archive?

Writeups force precision. They turn “I solved it once” into **“I can explain and reproduce it later”**, which is far more valuable once patterns start repeating across pwnable challenges and real engineering work.
